By Binomial Distribution, we have
\begin{align}
    \pr{X=k} &= \comb{^n}{C}{_k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}
\end{align}
Given $\pr{X=0}=\pr{X=1}$
\begin{align}
    \pr{X=0} &= \pr{X=1} \\
    \comb{^n}{C}{_0} p^0 (1-p)^{n-0} &= \comb{^n}{C}{_1} p^1 (1-p)^{n-1} \\
    (1-p)^{n} &= n p (1-p)^{n-1} \\
    (1-p) &= n p \\
    1 &= (1+n) p \\
    p &= \frac{1}{n+1}
\end{align}
Therefore, the value of $p$ is option (C) $\frac{1}{n+1}$
